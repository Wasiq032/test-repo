Biryani

Description

Biryani is a delicious rice dish peppered with scrumptious spices like saffron and cumin and further layered with spiced meat or veggie protein. Typically, this layered rice dish is enriched with added yogurt or ghee, a clarified butter, for an extra boost of luxurious flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Basmati rice
  • 500 g chicken or mutton
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a large pan. Add bay leaves, cloves, cardamom pods, and cumin seeds. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onions to the pan and cook until they turn golden brown.
  4. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the oil begins to separate.
  6. Add the chicken or mutton pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Mix in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Pour in the yogurt and cook until the meat is tender and cooked through, about 15-20 minutes.
  9. In a separate large pot, bring water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and the soaked, drained rice. Cook the rice until it is 70% cooked (it should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice.
  10. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat mixture in the pan.
  11. Sprinkle chopped cilantro and mint leaves over the rice.
  12. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
  13. Remove from heat and let it sit for 10 minutes before serving.
  14. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving. Enjoy!
